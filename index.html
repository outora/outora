Join A Real Orbital Space Mission

Our space program has been developed to allow anyone to gain experience in space technologies by being a part of a real life orbital cubesat mission

Get started in 3 steps

Join the mission and we'll be in touch with our mission schedule and milestones as well as an idea of what to expected from the program.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the Orbital Space Program cost?

The minimum cost to join the mission is $298 AUD for one timeslot on the mission. You can use our custom platform to raise this money in small amounts from your friends and family or sponsor the whole amount yourself or a combination of both. The more money you raise the more timeslots you will unlock. A timeslot should have a chance to run up to 12 times during the duration of the mission maximising your chance to get valuable data.

What sensors are on the mission payload?

We are still finalising the sensor layout of the mission but we can say with a high degree of certainty that we will have sensors to track ultraviolet light, magnetism, acceleration and temperature as well as an onboard camera and a radio transmitter. We will be adding more to this later. As a mission participant you will have access to program and use these sensors and the camera and transmitter during your timeslot on the mission.

Who else is on the mission with me and how do I connect with them?

This mission is shared between dozens of other interested parties, people who are really and truly interested in space. Some will be rockstar programmers and others will be new and enthusiastic. The goal is to share this experience together, something challenging and bigger than one individual. To help with this we will establish a private mission community group to help mission participants connect and help each other.

How do you guarantee quality of everyone's project?

We do this in three ways. First, our team will review and rigorously test every project added to the mission to ensure it will not cause problems for the other participants. Second we have designed the project in a way that separates out each person's code so a problem in one project will not cause problems for others. Finally, each participant will have access to and be encouraged to review the code of the other mission participants. It's only fair that we do so you can have peace of mind.

Can I track the mission after launch and how long is it expected to run?

We will be tracking the mission payload using our own network of ground stations as well as additional stations from mission participants and other supporters. We will do our best to get this data up onto the community portal where everyone can access it. We will also provide advice on setting up your own small ground station. Your mission timeslot should have a chance to run up to 12 times (depending on launch variables) during the duration of the mission maximising your chance to get the data you need.

Do I need any specific skills or knowledge to be a part of the mission?

No you don't. Our team can help you get up to scratch with the basic skills you need to be a part of the mission through online video content and 1 on 1 support. In fact, this space program is a great way to learn to program hardware. We will be providing the hardware information once the mission kicks off in 2017 so you can test on your own mini version of the payload. Don't forget, our goal is to make the mission successful so we'll do our best to bring you up to scratch and make that happen.
